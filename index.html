FIFA World Cup

The Ultimate Pinnacle of International Football

The 2026 Era

The 2026 FIFA World Cup marks a historic milestone as the largest tournament ever hosted. Jointly organized across 16 cities in Canada, Mexico, and the United States from June 11 to July 19, 2026, the tournament features an expanded field of 48 national teams playing a total of 104 matches.

Tournament History

Inaugurated in 1930, the FIFA World Cup has been held every four years, with exceptions in 1942 and 1946 due to World War II. It brings together national teams from every continent to compete for the prestigious gold trophy.

The Format

Teams qualify through rigorous continental tournaments organized by FIFA's six confederations. In the final tournament, teams are divided into groups for a round-robin stage, with the top contenders advancing to a high-stakes, single-elimination knockout bracket.
